# Material Specifications

## 1. Magneto-Optic Materials
The core sensing element requires materials with a high Verdet constant and low optical absorption at the operating wavelength.

### Primary Candidate: Bismuth-Substituted Yttrium Iron Garnet (BIG)
*   **Formula**: $\text{Bi}_x\text{Y}_{3-x}\text{Fe}_5\text{O}_{12}$
*   **Operating Wavelength**: 1550 nm (C-band)
*   **Refractive Index ($n$)**: ~2.3 - 2.4
*   **Dielectric Permittivity ($\epsilon$)**: ~6.25
*   **Gyrotropic Permittivity ($\epsilon_g$)**: ~0.06 (at saturation)
*   **Saturation Magnetic Field**: ~0.05 T (500 Gauss)
*   **Specific Faraday Rotation**: > 10,000 deg/cm (highly dependent on Bi substitution level)

### Alternative Candidate: Cerium-Substituted Yttrium Iron Garnet (Ce:YIG)
*   **Formula**: $\text{Ce}_x\text{Y}_{3-x}\text{Fe}_5\text{O}_{12}$
*   **Features**: Compatible with silicon photonics integration; high figure of merit.

## 2. Substrate Materials
*   **Fused Silica ($\text{SiO}_2$)**: High purity, low loss, refractive index ~1.44.
*   **Silicon (Si)**: For CMOS-compatible fabrication; refractive index ~3.48.
*   **Gadolinium Gallium Garnet (GGG)**: Lattice-matched substrate for high-quality epitaxial growth of BIG/YIG films.

## 3. Cladding/Superstrate
*   **Air**: Refractive index ~1.0.
*   **PMMA/Polymer**: For flexible skin applications; index ~1.49.
